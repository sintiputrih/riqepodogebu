What is Joe Namaths Net Worth: Biography,Net Worth &amp; more

Joe Namath is an American former football quarterback who has an estimated net worth of US$25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. He play for thirteen seasons in the American Football League (AFL) and National Football League (NFL), spending twelve years with New York Jets and one season with Los Angeles RAMS.

Namath’s wealth was accumulated primarily through his 13 seasons playing career. He earned substantial amounts from endorsement deals and his several business ventures.

Who is Joe Namath?

Joseph William Namath was born on May 31, 1943 in Beaver Falls, Pennsylvania in the United States. His parents were Rose Juhász and John Andrew Namath, both of whom were staunch Roman Catholics with Hungarian descent. His father was a steelworker.

During his childhood, he was really close with both his parents and had to stay with his mother after his parents divorced. Namath has four siblings, three older biological brothers and an adopted older sister.

Namath attended Beaver Fall High School, where he excelled in sports and was considered a standout performer at quarterback in football, guard in basketball and outfielder in baseball. Once on The James Brown Show, Namath revealed that he was the only white player on his high school basketball team. He also made dunks in an era when it was rarely seen in high school basketball.

Cartilage damage to Namath’s knee made him ineligible for the draft during the Vietnam war, and he was signed by the New York Jets (who then played in the AFL). In 1965, he was given the nickname “Broadway Joe” after appearing on the cover of “Sports Illustrated.” During his first season, Namath was named AFL Rookie of the Year. Despite this, he continued to struggle with knee injuries.

Shortly before the NFL and the AFL were merged into one, Joe Namath played the game of his life during Super Bowl III, beating the Baltimore Colts after personally guaranteeing the victory days earlier. He became the MVP of the Superbowl game, completing eight passes to one player alone (George Sauer) for 133 yards.

Over the next period, Namath struggled with injuries and began to miss games for the first time in his career. However, Joe continued to wow audiences, throwing for 496 yards in a victory against the Colts.

Many experts consider this to be the finest example of passing in a single game. Towards the end of his career, he transferred to the Los Angeles Rams. Numerous injuries prevented him from finishing on a high, and he retired at the end of the 1977 season.
