Carly Pearce - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Divorced, Career, Facts

Table of Biography

Carly Pearce is an American singer and songwriter who specializes in country music. On October 13, 2017, Carly Pearce released her first studio album, ‘Every Little Thing.’

Early life

Carly Pearce was born on 24th April 1990, in Taylor Mill, Kentucky, the United States of America. Her birth name is  Carly Cristyne Slusser. As of now, this singer is 32 years old and holds American nationality.

In addition, her zodiac sign is Taurus and her ethical background is unknown. She is the daughter of Todd Slusser(father) and Jackie Slusser(mother).

Moreover, she has a sister whose name is Cristy. She grew up in her hometown, where her grandparents established in her a love of country music by playing it at home regularly when she was a child.

Awards

Caption: Carly Pearce receiving CMA award (Source: Taste of Country)

In 2018, she received the CMT Music Award in the category of Breakthrough Video of the Year. Additionally, she earned the Country Music Association Award for Musical Event of the Year in 2020.

In the same year, she received two Academy of Country Music Awards for Single of the Year and Music Event of the Year. Moreover, she was inducted as a member of the Grand Ole Opry in 2021.

In the same year, she won the Country Music Association Award for Female Vocalist of the Year. Furthermore, she received the Academy of Country Music Awards for Female Artist of the Year and Music Event of the Year in 2022.

Professional career

Carly began singing professionally when she was 11 years old. She performed with a Bluegrass band regularly, as well as in church and tent revival concerts.

Moreover, she auditioned for the “Country Crossroads” show at the age of 16 and even dropped out of high school to be a part of it, where she performed five times a week. This singer later moved to Nashville, Tennessee, when she was 19 years old to pursue a career in country music.

In 2012, at the age of 22, she got a developmental agreement with Sony Music Nashville, but it was quickly canceled. She did several part-time jobs after that, including cleaning Airbnbs.

Carly played at the Grand Ole Opry in 2015 after making contact with Pete Fisher, the Grand Ole Opry’s vice president, and general manager. Busbee signed her to a developing artist agreement the same year, and she was featured on the track “Wasn’t That Drunk” later that year.

After the launch of her debut single, “Every Little Thing,” in February 2017, she had a breakthrough career.

The song was a huge hit, peaking at number five on the Billboard Hot Country Songs chart and number one on the Billboard Country Airplay chart. Her debut studio album, “Every Little Thing,” was released on October 13, 2017.

She then went on multiple tours with Luke Bryan, Thomas Rhett, and Blake Shelton, as well as other country performers. “Hide the Wine,” her second major hit single was released later that year and reached number 13 on the Billboard Country Airplay chart.

Another song, “Closer to You,” was released in November 2018. She released a second song from her unreleased second studio album, “I Hope You’re Happy Now,” in October 2019.

Her self-titled second studio album, “Carly Pearce,” releases on February 14, 2020, and debuts at Number 6 on the Top Country Albums chart and Number 73 on the Billboard 200 chart.
