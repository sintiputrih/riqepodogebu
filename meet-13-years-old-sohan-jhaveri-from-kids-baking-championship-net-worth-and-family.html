Meet Sohan Jhaveri, a 13-year-old contestant in the Kids Baking Championship

Sohan Jhaveri, as previously said, is a chef, entrepreneur, and the creator of SoJo Kitchen & Co. With his mother’s full backing, the eighth-grader is also the youngest member of the Denver Metro Chamber of Commerce.

According to his company website, Sohan researched and manufactured pastry favorites such as macarons, eclairs, other baked goods, and chocolates, motivated by his enthusiasm for studying traditional baking techniques and chocolatier. By incorporating odd tastes and mixes into his creations, the youthful chef merges his passion of baking with his South Asian and Indian background.

Sohan Jhaveri and other Kids Baking Championship season 11 competitors. (Photo courtesy of Food Network)SoJo Kitchen & Company was founded with the help of friends and family. Sohan Jhaveri, 13, started the Company in the midst of the worldwide epidemic in the summer of 2020.

As an artisan baker, Sohan makes everything in tiny amounts. His pastries, bakery items, chocolates, macarons, and other treats are all masterfully produced and carefully chosen for their flavor, quality, and sweetness.

Parents and Family of Sohan Jhaveri

Sohan Jhaveri was born in 2009 and will be 13 in 2022. The Colorado native was greeted by his mother, Neelima, and his father, the identity of whom was not revealed. Sohan’s mother fully supports his company and ambitions.In an interview, the adolescent baker’s mother said that it was all Sohan’s fault; he started watching YouTube videos and reading books. He perused the Julia Child book that Neelima had given him.

Furthermore, Sohan is said to have been reared with his parents’ business-oriented attitudes. During the same interview, the young chef mentioned baking and admiring his business-savvy parents during networking meals. Although Jhaveri’s mother has been his staunchest supporter, she chooses to remain out of the spotlight. Her Instagram account is operational, but her photographs are secret.

Sohan Jhaveri’s Journey: How He Made His Passion A Real Business

Sohan Jhaveri spent most of his youth in the kitchen with his grandmother. He started experimenting in the kitchen as a passionate baker after watching YouTube and reading Julia Child’s book. Sohan finally perfected the macaron after several days in the kitchen.

The initial batches were sold to his friends and family. Following that, he began selling his things on Etsy, and his company grew. Sohan currently offers macarons as well as other delicacies such as eclairs, cakes, cupcakes, chocolate, and more on his website.

Sohan also said that he wants to be a member in order to promote his brand and company. Despite the difficulties of balancing business and education, he is succeeding. We wish him the best of luck in his future undertakings.
