Bob Stoops: A Legendary Figure in Oklahoma Football

By Jocelyn Alano July 03, 2024 21:27

credit. Jonathan Bachman/Getty Images

Recently, however, Bob Stoops - an Oklahoma football legend in his own right, has made headlines after speaking out about the UFL and NFL as well as what he believes is wrong with college football today. Here we delve into his career, recent activities, and how he has shaped the sport.


The Legacy of Bob Stoops


Stoops served as the head coach of college football powerhouse Oklahoma from 1999 until his abrupt retirement in June. The Sooners won several conference titles and garnered a NCAA National Championship under his leadership in 2000. Stoops also won the program two Heisman Trophy winners and multiple prestigious bowl games, etching his name in Oklahoma lore forever.


Recent Engagements


Stoops served as head coach of the Dallas Renegades in 2020 and will become the team's first-ever head coach when they take part in their inaugural season in 2024. And in addition to that, for this rebuilding year no less: Drake Stoops - Bob's son who didn't get drafted a few days ago when the 2024 NFL Draft weekend went awry (and you'll see exactly how prejudiced everyone can act toward family insinuations here) - has signed as an undrafted free agent with none other than the Los Angeles Rams.


College Ball

Stoops has spoken openly about his misgivings with the current regime in college football. He's questioned the viability of the transfer portal, and whether or not athletes will be able to jump to other schools in free agency under NIL ruless. Stoops calls for college football to have a commissioner, and explains why governance is overdue.


Looking Ahead

Decried as negative and cynical, Stoops is still a radical figure in the football world. In the UFL, with his son's NFL hopes, and opining on what college football could look like a decade from now at least here the Stoops name still carries some weight in all things about football.


In the End

The career of Bob Stoops as well as his recent endeavors display a passion for the game of football ingrained deep. He also left a legacy at Oklahoma and his current contributions to the sport showcase how his impact will never be forgotten. Bob Stoops is the most interesting man in football still an influential figure whether he's on college football sidelines or advocating for change.
Bob Stoops' journey through the game serves as a perfect embodiment of everything football has been while remaining an example of what it is.
