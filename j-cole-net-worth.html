Charity

Cole is the kind of person who likes to give back to the community. He has not overlooked where he came from and knows how it feels to not have.

First and foremost, J Cole has his own charitable foundation named Dreamville Foundation, a non-profit organization with the objective to provide special opportunities to the youths within his hometown of Fayetteville, North Carolina.

The foundation is primarily funded by his own record label Dreamville Records.

Similarly, He lends his house in his hometown, where he lived as a child to struggling families without rent for two years, and again after the previous family leaves the house, he lends to some other struggling family.

Likewise, Cole donated $10,000 to Baltimore Organization as part of the Colin Kaepernick charity pledge in 2018.

Career

Cole has always had a thing for basketball and music since his childhood. He started rapping when he was twelve years of age.

Moreover, He saw music as an ideal profession in 2000 after his mother bought an ASR-X musical sampler as a Christmas present.

Cole was a bright student too. He graduated High School with a 4.2 GPA and then moved to New York City to secure a recording contract.

Furthermore, He went to St. John’s University, initially majoring in Computer Science and later switching to communication and business after witnessing a lonely computer science professor.

He graduated magna cum laude with a 3.8 GPA in 2007.

However, the university held back from granting a degree because he had owed money for a library book.

He ultimately received a degree in 2015 during a homecoming concert.
