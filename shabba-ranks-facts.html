Age

Born on January 17, 1966, Shabba Ranks is currently 55 years old. He was born in Sturgetown, Jamaica, and grew up in the rough Garrison area of Kingston.

Career

Shabba Ranks began his musical career in the 1980s as a DJ on local sound systems. He gained popularity in Jamaica with his unique style of chanting and toasting on the mic, often rapping in patois, the Jamaican dialect. He soon caught the attention of record producers and was signed to the label Jammys Records. His debut single “Heat Under Sufferers Feet” was a hit in Jamaica, and he soon followed up with other hits like “Roots and Culture” and “Wicked Inna Bed.” These songs became anthems for Jamaica’s dancehall scene and established Shabba Ranks as one of the pioneers of the genre.

Shabba Ranks’ success in Jamaica paved the way for him to international stardom. In the early 1990s, he signed with Epic Records, and his first international album, “As Raw As Ever,” was released in 1991. The album featured the hit song “Housecall,” which was a collaboration with American R&B singer Maxi Priest. The song became a massive success, hitting number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, and earning Shabba Ranks his first Grammy award for Best Reggae Album.

Shabba Ranks continued to release hit albums during the 1990s, including “X-Tra Naked” and “Rough and Ready Vol. I,” which featured collaborations with some of the biggest names in hip-hop, including KRS-One, Queen Latifah, and Chubb Rock.
